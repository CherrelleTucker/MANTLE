# Naming History

How we landed on **KITCHEN**.

---

## The chosen name

**KITCHEN** — *Knowledge, Interviews, Transitions, Cookbooks, Handoffs, Equivalencies, Network*

Tagline: ***Let's get Cooking.***

The cookbook is the platform's defining artifact: every Project Coordinator writes one at handoff, and the platform's centerpiece feature compiles it from structured data. Naming the platform KITCHEN puts the metaphor on the front door — recipes are written, ingredients are gathered, the cookbook is assembled. The name does communication work the moment someone hears it: no explanation needed for what it produces or why it exists.

---

## Constraints

- **Must not collide with a NASA mission name.** NASA's naming office reserves mission names; using one for an internal tool creates confusion and can be rejected.
- **Must be easy to say and spell.**
- **Should fit the metaphor of the platform's purpose** (transition, knowledge handoff, continuity).
- **Should align loosely with Barrios's brand themes** (mission-driven, partnership, heritage, "passionately advancing humanity").
- **Backronym-friendly** — letters should map naturally to platform content without forcing.

---

## Alternatives considered

### Eliminated due to NASA mission collision
| Name | Mission collision |
|------|-------------------|
| PACE | NASA Earth-observing satellite (Plankton, Aerosol, Cloud, ocean Ecosystem) |
| ATLAS | ULA rocket family + multiple NASA missions |
| RELAY | Early NASA communications satellite (1960s) |
| HORIZON | New Horizons probe |
| COURIER | Courier 1B early US comm satellite |

### Considered, ranked alongside KITCHEN
| Name | Metaphor | Why not chosen |
|------|----------|----------------|
| **CAIRN** | Trail markers stacked by predecessors so successors find the way | Excellent metaphor (asynchronous, additive, multi-generational) but requires explanation; KITCHEN is more immediately legible |
| **COMPASS** | Guidance, orientation | Common, slightly corporate; less distinctive |
| **BRIDGE** | Connects departing to arriving | Solid but generic |
| **ANCHOR** | Stability through change | Reasonable but less specific to handoff |
| **LEGACY** | Heritage and preservation | Strong fit with Barrios's 45-year emphasis, but "Y" is a hard letter to backronym |
| **NEXUS** | Connection point | Slightly cold/technical; less human than KITCHEN |
| **TRELLIS** | A structure that supports growth | Interesting but obscure |

---

## Backronym derivation

Letters chosen to map cleanly to the actual content stored in KITCHEN:

| Letter | Word | Maps to |
|--------|------|---------|
| **K** | Knowledge | Tier 1 universal PC knowledge, the wiki, decisions log |
| **I** | Interviews | Working Styles Matrix intake (per-PC and per-Stakeholder) |
| **T** | Transitions | The whole purpose of the platform |
| **C** | Cookbooks | The Word export — the centerpiece feature |
| **H** | Handoffs | Living handover, quick capture, asynchronous predecessor/successor flow |
| **E** | Equivalencies | The 3-axis Equivalency Map (Slack to Teams, Asana to Planner, etc.) |
| **N** | Network | Stakeholders List + Meetings catalog |

No forced letters. Each component corresponds to a real piece of the data model.
